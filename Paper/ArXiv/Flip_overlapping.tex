\begin{abstract}
The companion paper~\cite{companion} measured how much of a language model's
truth representation fits on a single training-free direction, and where that
reading fails. This paper asks the two questions underneath: \emph{which
component of the transformer makes the axis, and what is the axis made of?}
On four base models across two families (Qwen2.5-1.5B/3B, Llama-3.2-1B/3B),
under held-out protocols with pre-registered predictions, we report three
findings. First, a \emph{relational law}: the feed-forward network's
contribution at a block is anti-aligned with every truth frame that does not
contain it, while attention is pro-aligned with frames it did not write; at
the peak the two components are near mirror images ($+1.63$ vs.\ $-1.43$ on
Llama-3.2-3B). We name the asymmetry \emph{propagate vs.\ overwrite}. The law
generalizes, and corrects, an earlier peak-anchored reading of our own, whose
circularity was exposed by an axis-provenance control we built against our
own instrument; the correction is reported as a result. Second, the genuine
\emph{erosion} of the truth signal after the peak survives the correction and
is causally attributed to the SwiGLU value stream, not the gate. Third, the
axis is a \emph{semantically signed mixture}: per-category truth axes form a
structured geometry (language relations cohere, continent anti-aligns,
near-antiparallel language axes merge at the peak), the FFN writes truth
along category-dependent directions ($74$--$82\%$ category decoding vs.\
$39$--$42\%$ lexical baseline), and the peak arrangement converges across the
two families (Mantel $r = +0.77$, $p = 0.0009$) while contradicting the
shared lexical arrangement both start from. The mixture's geometry is a
property of the knowledge domain, not of the training recipe. A measured
dictionary and a four-criterion contract for any future sparse autoencoder
are delivered as the engineering handoff.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

A training-free direction in the residual stream of a small language model
carries a real, held-out, permutation-tested truth signal on facts the model
knows; that signal is bounded by a full linear probe, and the gap between the
two grows as the model's knowledge decreases and as the material becomes
heterogeneous~\cite{companion}. Those are measurements about the axis as an
object. This paper is about the axis as a \emph{process} and as a
\emph{composite}: which architectural component builds it, which degrades it,
by what mechanism, and what internal structure the single direction is
summarizing.

The paper proceeds in two movements. The first movement is mechanical.
Starting from the causal finding of the companion paper (attention builds the
signal in the middle layers, the FFN degrades it after the peak), we sharpen
the instrument from per-band ablation to a per-layer, per-component,
fixed-frame projection, and follow it across four models and two families.
The initial result, confirmed by pre-registered prediction on three models,
was striking and simple: the FFN writes pro-truth into the peak block and
flips to anti-truth at exactly the next block, wherever the peak sits. That
result was then subjected to a control of our own construction, fitting the
measuring frame at several blocks rather than only at the peak, and the
control exposed a circularity in the simple reading. What replaced it is
better: a fully relational law in which the FFN opposes every truth frame it
did not help write and attention propagates every frame it did not write,
with the peak-anchored flip as the special case where the frame is fitted at
the peak. Section~\ref{sec:correction} narrates the failure and the
generalization in full, because the sequence (claim, self-raised objection,
pre-registered discrimination, exposure, stronger law) is scientific content,
not an embarrassment to be footnoted.

The second movement is geometric. If the FFN writes truth in
category-dependent directions, the single axis of the companion paper cannot
be an atom; it must be a mixture. Grouping the data by Wikidata relation and
fitting per-category truth axes, we measure the mixture directly: its signed
geometry, its reorganization with depth, and, in a registered cross-family
falsification, the convergence of its arrangement between the Qwen and Llama
families. The heterogeneity effect measured in the companion paper (the probe
gap jumping from $0.062$ on homogeneous material to $0.203$ on a mix) is,
in hindsight, this mixture seen from one direction.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}
\item The \emph{relational law} (pre-registered, four models, two families):
FFN contributions anti-align with truth frames that do not contain them;
attention contributions pro-align with frames they did not write; at the
peak, under a clean pre-FFN frame, the two are near mirror images
(Sec.~\ref{sec:law}).
\item The \emph{axis-provenance control} that forced the law: a
ruler-placement method with synthetic ground-truth validation that separates
genuine alignment from measurement circularity, applied against our own
published claim (Secs.~\ref{sec:provenance},~\ref{sec:correction}).
\item Causal attribution of the frame-free \emph{erosion} to the SwiGLU
value stream, by an exact algebraic split of the FFN's along-axis write and
by causal freezes, with the gate exonerated (Sec.~\ref{sec:erosion}).
\item A pre-registered \emph{negative}: the gauge-invariant OV/QK circuit
crossover does not track the truth peak across scale
(Sec.~\ref{sec:negatives}).
\item The \emph{category geometry}: per-category truth axes form a
semantically signed arrangement that reorganizes with depth and converges
across families (Mantel $r = +0.77$, $p = 0.0009$), with the FFN's write
directions decoding the category far above a lexical baseline
(Sec.~\ref{sec:mixture}).
\item A \emph{measured dictionary} and a four-criterion registered contract
that any trained sparse autoencoder must beat to justify its cost
(Sec.~\ref{sec:contract}).
\end{enumerate}

\section{Related work}

\paragraph{Truth directions and their limits.} The companion
paper~\cite{companion} defines the axis, the held-out protocol, the
permutation null, and the know-rate used throughout, and situates the
approach against linear probes and sparse dictionaries; those definitions are
reused here, not restated. B\"urger et al.~\cite{burger2024} establish the
two-dimensional truth subspace and the polarity direction whose unsupervised
recovery the companion paper reports.

\paragraph{MLPs as key-value writers.} The memory-editing
literature~\cite{meng2022} attributes to transformer MLPs the physiology of
key-value stores whose writes deposit factual content into the residual
stream. The relational law of Sec.~\ref{sec:law} is a dynamical face of that
picture: the component that deposits the next frame's material is, per these
measurements, the one that opposes the current frame.

\paragraph{Rotational dynamics of factual selection.} A recent
preprint~\cite{rotational2026} reports that factual constraint processing
separates alternatives by rotation rather than rescaling, with active
suppression of the correct continuation at intermediate depth. The
propagate/overwrite asymmetry supplies a candidate microscopic writer for
that macroscopic observation.

\paragraph{Attention-head specialization.} The head-taxonomy line of
work~\cite{headgenome} motivated our pre-registered test of whether static,
gauge-invariant circuit norms predict where truth lives (they do not,
Sec.~\ref{sec:negatives}) and defines the natural per-head follow-up of
Sec.~\ref{sec:leads}.

\paragraph{Cross-model representation convergence.} The convergence of the
category arrangement across families (Sec.~\ref{sec:mixture}) is a signed,
truth-specific instance of the broader phenomenon that different models
trained on the same world converge to similar
representations~\cite{platonic2024}.

\section{Scope, evidence levels, and the status of a correction}
\label{sec:scope}

Nothing here has the status of a settled result; everything is labeled.
Results are organized in three evidential levels and never mixed.
\textbf{Level A}: replicated across models with predictions written before
the runs (the relational law, the dimensionality points, the crossover
negative, the arrangement law). \textbf{Level B}: single-model but causal,
with multiple independent methods agreeing (the value-stream attribution).
\textbf{Level C}: leads, declared as such (Sec.~\ref{sec:leads}).

This document also contains a correction of our own published
interpretation, reported as a result. The original peak-anchored reading of
the FFN flip was confirmed by pre-registered prediction on three models and
then exposed as the special case of a relational law by a control we built
against our own instrument. Every v1 number reproduces; what changed is what
one of them meant. Three further items from earlier stages are retracted or
falsified below and reported in full: the ``attention builds, FFN
consolidates'' story (overturned causally in~\cite{companion}); the
hypothesis that the SwiGLU gate carries the erosion (falsified in favor of
the value stream, Sec.~\ref{sec:erosion}); and the hypothesis that the
static OV/QK crossover predicts the truth peak (falsified by pre-registered
criterion, Sec.~\ref{sec:negatives}). A fourth item, the ``FFN-off beats
frozen'' margin, failed to replicate across scale and is demoted from lead
to noise.

\section{Methods}
\label{sec:methods}

\subsection{The fixed-axis contribution gap}
\label{sec:fixedaxis}

Within a block the residual stream updates additively,
$h_{b+1} = h_b + a_b + f_b$, with $a_b$ the attention contribution and $f_b$
the FFN contribution, captured by forward hooks~\cite{companion}. The axis
$v_1$ is fit once, on training folds only, on a chosen \emph{fit block}
(oriented true $=$ positive there), and every contribution at every layer is
projected onto that same fixed direction. The statistic for a contribution
$c$ is the held-out class gap
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{gap}(c) \;=\; \overline{(v_1 \cdot c)}\big|_{\mathrm{true}}
\;-\; \overline{(v_1 \cdot c)}\big|_{\mathrm{false}},
\qquad
d' \;=\; \frac{\mathrm{gap}}{\text{pooled std}},
\end{equation}
where the pooled standard deviation is computed over the held-out
projections of both classes. A negative FFN gap means the FFN literally
writes toward the false side of the axis on which the model represents
truth. Stability criterion (fixed in code before the consolidation runs): a
per-layer value is ``stable'' if its magnitude exceeds both $2\sigma$ across
$5$ CV seeds and a floor of $0.03$.

\subsection{The exact SwiGLU split}
\label{sec:swiglu}

The FFN computes $f = W_{\mathrm{down}}(g \odot u)$ with
$g = \mathrm{silu}(W_{\mathrm{gate}} x)$ and $u = W_{\mathrm{up}} x$,
elementwise in the shared expanded basis. The projection of $f$ on $v_1$ is
$w \cdot (g \odot u)$ with $w = W_{\mathrm{down}}^{\top} v_1$: the residual
axis pulled back through the down-projection. No axis is ever fit in the
$8960$/$11008$-dimensional expanded space, avoiding the $p \gg n$ regime
entirely. With the symmetric intra-pair split
($\Delta g = g_t - g_f$, $\bar{g} = (g_t + g_f)/2$, and likewise for $u$)
the mixed term cancels and the identity is exact:
\begin{equation}
w \cdot (g_t \odot u_t - g_f \odot u_f)
\;=\;
\underbrace{w \cdot (\Delta g \odot \bar{u})}_{\text{gate term}}
\;+\;
\underbrace{w \cdot (\bar{g} \odot \Delta u)}_{\text{value term}}.
\label{eq:split}
\end{equation}
Verified numerically at $7.4 \times 10^{-8}$ relative error on real data.

\subsection{Interventions}
\label{sec:interventions}

Beyond zero-ablation of a component over a band of layers, two refinements.
(i) A \emph{frozen control} obtained for free: with both attention and FFN
zeroed over the band, the readout state is identically the band-entry state,
so the frozen reference is read off the intact pass. (ii)
\emph{Gate-freeze / value-freeze}: at the reading position, $g$ (resp.\ $u$)
is replaced by its intra-pair mean cached from the intact run, killing the
class-driven variation of one stream while the FFN stays on. Zeroing the
gate outright would zero the whole FFN and test nothing new.

\subsection{Static circuit norms}
\label{sec:circuits}

Per attention head (GQA-aware: each group of query heads shares one K/V),
the gauge-invariant circuit norms $\lVert W_q^{\top} W_k \rVert_F$ (routing)
and $\lVert W_o W_v \rVert_F$ (writing)~\cite{elhage2021}. Individual weight
norms are gauge-dependent ($W_q \to \alpha W_q$, $W_k \to W_k/\alpha$ leaves
the function unchanged); the products are not. A gauge self-test (rescale
and verify invariance at machine precision) gates the computation.

\subsection{The axis-provenance control}
\label{sec:provenance}

The objection this method answers, raised by the author against the author's
own instrument, is that a result anchored to ``the block where the ruler was
fit, plus one'' could be a property of the ruler rather than of the FFN. The
control: fit $v_1$ at several blocks $b \in \{p{-}4, p{-}2, p, p{+}2\}$ and
recompute the full gap profile under each ruler. Two rulers per fit block,
from the same cached tensors:
\begin{align*}
\textbf{post}:&\quad v_1 \text{ fit on the block output }
h_{b+1} = h_b + a_b + f_b \quad (\text{contains } f_b),\\
\textbf{pre}:&\quad v_1 \text{ fit on the pre-FFN state }
h_b + a_b \quad (f_b \text{ excluded}).
\end{align*}
The pre ruler removes the direct circularity of measuring $f_b$ against an
axis fit on a state that contains $f_b$. The discrimination criterion was
fixed before the runs and validated on synthetic ground truth: a planted
global axis with a planted writer flip stays pinned under every ruler; a
planted circular writer collapses under the pre ruler (collapse ratio
$0.04$) while planted genuine alignment survives ($0.99$). Genuine alignment
therefore predicts a clearly positive pre-ruler diagonal; circularity
predicts collapse. A methodological note learned the hard way: two
successive automatic ``transition-rule'' verdicts in the tooling misread
these matrices during the analysis; the released tool prints the full
matrices and diagonal summaries with \emph{no} automatic verdict, and every
reading below is of the full matrices.

\subsection{Per-category axes, transfer, decoding, and the Mantel statistic}
\label{sec:catmethods}

CounterFact is grouped by Wikidata relation; the top-$K$ relations by unique
pairs are used ($K{=}5$ exploratory, $K{=}8$ for the registered
falsification; $60$ pairs each): P103 mother tongue, P1412 languages spoken,
P176 manufacturer, P27 citizenship, P30 continent, P37 official language,
P413 sport position, P495 country of origin. Per-category truth axes are fit
at the peak block, each oriented true $=$ positive \emph{within its own
category}, so the sign of a cross-category cosine is information: negative
means the shared direction reads the other category's truth backwards. Four
measurements: (i) signed cosine matrices at the peak and at an early block
(block $2$, the lexical surface control); (ii) the held-out \emph{transfer
matrix}, whose cell $(i,j)$ is the held-out AUC of category $i$'s axis
applied to category $j$'s pairs; (iii) nearest-centroid \emph{decoding} of
the category from the FFN's class-signed write direction $\Delta f$ at
peak$+1$, against the same decoder on early residual differences (the
lexical baseline); (iv) the \emph{Mantel test}, a permutation test for the
correlation between two distance (here: signed-cosine) matrices, used to
compare the two families' arrangements under category-label permutation.
Deterministic sampling per relation was verified by exact reproduction of
the $K{=}5$ submatrix inside the $K{=}8$ run; a determinism defect in the
decoding fold assignment (salted string hash) was found by cross-run
comparison and fixed (process-stable CRC32) before the canonical runs.

\section{The correction: from peak-anchored to relational}
\label{sec:correction}

The sequence is narrated in three acts because the sequence is the content.

\paragraph{Act 1 (the claim).} The FFN gap, measured under the peak-fit
ruler, is pro-truth at $p$ and anti-truth at $p{+}1$, replicated with
pre-registered predictions on three models across two families
(Sec.~\ref{sec:flip}).

\paragraph{Act 2 (the control bites its owner).} Under rulers fit at
$p{-}4$, $p{-}2$, $p$, $p{+}2$ (Sec.~\ref{sec:provenance}), the transition
does not stay at $p{+}1$: \emph{it tracks the ruler}. On Qwen2.5-1.5B the
diagonal reads pro at $b$ / anti at $b{+}1$ for $b = 13, 15, 17$ alike
($+0.61/-0.79$, $+0.57/-0.82$, $+0.52/-0.73$); on Qwen2.5-3B for
$b = 16, 18$; and subsequently on both Llamas (Table~\ref{tab:post}). The
peak-anchored flip was the $b{=}p$ slice of a relational pattern present at
every block where an axis exists.

\paragraph{Act 3 (the pre ruler exposes the circularity).} Under the
pre-FFN ruler the ``pro at own block'' term does not merely collapse; it
\emph{inverts sign} (Table~\ref{tab:pre}). Measured against the frame that
exists at the moment it writes, the FFN's contribution is strongly
anti-aligned. The pro term of the post ruler was, in substantial part, the
ruler measuring its own ingredient: $f_b$ is inside $h_{b+1}$ and drags the
axis fit there toward itself. The residual above-diagonal positives under
the pre ruler (e.g.\ $f_{13}$ at $+0.48$/$+0.60$ on pre-frames of blocks
$15$/$17$ in Qwen-1.5B) are the same circularity one step forward, since
those later frames contain $f_{13}$.

What the failure bought: a general law instead of a special case, a measured
mechanism instead of a coordinate, and a live demonstration that the shields
bind their owner. Where the axis is weak or absent, the pattern correctly
vanishes: rulers fit before the Qwen-3B ignition (fit-block cosines to the
peak axis $0.049$ and $0.079$) and before the Llama-1B mid-knowledge peak
return noise, the internal consistency expected if the law is about frames
that exist.

(Llama-3B). Pre-registered predictions for the two Llamas, written before
those runs (POST pro/anti diagonal; PRE sign inversion for the FFN;
sub-diagonal positive for attention and negative for the FFN), were all
confirmed.

We name the asymmetry \textbf{propagate vs.\ overwrite}. Attention, which
reads the residual stream, transports the truth frames already present: it
is pro-aligned with directions it did not write. The FFN, which generates
content from its weights, opposes the frame of its moment and deposits the
material of the next: its writes are what later frames are made of. The
correct ontology dissolves the earlier implicit picture of an axis being
eroded like an object: the axis is not an object in the model; it is a
moving statistical summary of where the class difference lives, and the
FFN's class-signed writes constitute it. This is the dynamical face of the
key-value write-head physiology attributed to MLPs by the memory-editing
literature~\cite{meng2022}, and it supplies a candidate microscopic writer
for the macroscopic finding of~\cite{rotational2026} that factual selection
separates by rotation with active suppression of the correct continuation.

\section{Level B: the erosion is real, frame-free, and carried by the value stream}
\label{sec:erosion}

The correction separates two phenomena that the peak-anchored reading
conflated: \emph{frame-relative rotation} (what the flip actually was) and
\emph{frame-free decay} of class signal after the peak (the genuine
erosion). The erosion stands on measurements that use no frozen ruler.

First, the refit truth AUC (axis re-fit at every layer) decays after the
peak (e.g.\ $0.79 \to 0.54$ on Qwen-1.5B): if the FFN only rotated the frame
while preserving class signal, refit readability would not fall. Second, the
causal results: zero-ablating the FFN over the post-peak band raises refit
AUC ($+0.103$ on Qwen-1.5B, band $16$--$18$; $+0.053$ on Qwen-3B, band
$17$--$19$), attention-off does not, and the axis-geometry cosines show it
is the FFN that rotates the readable direction (intact entry$\to$readout
$|\cos|$ $0.627$/$0.667$ vs.\ $0.876$/$0.890$ with the FFN off). Two honest
annotations. The earlier lead ``FFN-off beats the frozen reference'' did not
replicate ($+0.022$ on 1.5B vs.\ $+0.015$ on 3B, both straddling the noise
threshold); the supported statement is FFN-off $\approx$ frozen on both
models. And a scale difference: in the 3B, attention-off in the band hurts
($-0.055$), consistent with its post-peak attention still writing pro-truth
(fixed-axis gap $+0.663$ at block $18$): the 3B erosion band is a
tug-of-war, not a passive decline.

\paragraph{Inside the SwiGLU: the value stream, not the gate.} The working
hypothesis entering this chapter was contextual: the gate (which neurons
open) switches the stream toward next-token format context and thereby
erodes truth. Three independent methods return the same verdict, and it is
the opposite attribution. (i) The exact split of Eq.~\ref{eq:split} on
Qwen2.5-1.5B:

true vs.\ $-1.65$ false). The corrected statement: after the peak, the FFN's
value stream injects prediction-oriented content whose along-axis component
is systematically anti-truth. This attribution is causal on one model; the
observational split table should be re-read against pre-FFN rulers
(Sec.~\ref{sec:leads}), but the causal freezes are frame-free and stand.

\paragraph{The rotation objection, raised and closed.} Could the negative
gap be a rotating write-direction seen through a fixed axis, a projection
artifact, rather than genuine anti-truth writing? Rotation makes a precise
prediction: the cosine of the mean FFN pair-difference with $v_1$ should
sweep smoothly through zero across several layers with roughly constant
magnitude. The data show the opposite on both consolidated models: a
one-step sign jump at peak$+1$ with the magnitude intact (Qwen-3B:
$\cos = +0.245 \to -0.409$, $\Delta = -0.654$, $\lVert d \rVert$
$2.52 \to 2.34$; Llama-1B: $+0.292 \to -0.341$, $\Delta = -0.632$,
$\lVert d \rVert$ $0.33 \to 0.34$). It is class-signed writing, not
rotation. A secondary reading of the same table: most of what the FFN
writes is orthogonal to the axis (class-blind volume), with a small, real,
systematically anti-truth along-axis component on top.

\section{Pre-registered negatives, and ignition as a family trait}
\label{sec:negatives}

\paragraph{The static OV/QK crossover does not track the peak.} Motivated by
an external observation that value-to-query weight-norm ratios scale with
depth across architectures~\cite{headgenome}, we tested the gauge-invariant
version: does the layer where the writing circuit $\lVert W_o W_v \rVert$
overtakes the routing circuit $\lVert W_q^{\top} W_k \rVert$
(median-normalized, sustained for $3$ consecutive layers, criterion fixed in
code before the runs) predict the truth peak? On Qwen2.5-1.5B the crossover
(layer $17$) sits one block from the flip: consistent. The cross-scale
prediction registered before the second run discriminated two hypotheses:
peak tracks crossover (3B peak at $\sim 22 \pm 2$) vs.\ fixed relative depth
(peak at $\sim 19$). The measured 3B peak is block $16$, eight blocks from
its crossover ($24$); across the two scales the crossover moves deeper in
relative depth ($0.61 \to 0.67$) while the peak moves shallower
($0.54 \to 0.44$). Two points moving in opposite directions falsify the
link. The depth-scaling of the circuits themselves may well be real and
general; it is simply not where truth lives. The late-layer OV explosion
(e.g.\ $\lVert W_o W_v \rVert$ sums of $338.9$/$322.0$ at Qwen-1.5B layers
$26$--$27$) instead matches the decoupling of Sec.~\ref{sec:leads}.

\paragraph{Ignition is a family trait, not a scale effect.} The Qwen-3B
per-layer AUC curve switches on in a single layer ($0.526 \to 0.790$),
driven by a coordinated two-layer construction burst in which both
components write pro-truth strongly (attention $d'$ $+1.58$/$+1.27$ at
blocks $15$--$16$); the 1.5B curve is a smooth ramp. The pre-registered
discrimination: scale predicts a step in Llama-3.2-3B; family predicts a
ramp. Measured: a ramp ($0.526 \to 0.657 \to 0.664 \to 0.749 \to 0.785$
across levels $6$--$10$). Ignition is, on present evidence, a trait of the
Qwen recipe at the 3B scale, not of scale itself.

\section{Level A, second movement: the axis is a semantically signed mixture}
\label{sec:mixture}

If the FFN writes truth along directions that depend on \emph{what kind of
fact} is being written, the single axis is a mixture, and its ingredients
can be measured. The design and statistics are those of
Sec.~\ref{sec:catmethods}; three models carry this section (Qwen2.5-1.5B/3B
exploratory, Qwen2.5-3B and Llama-3.2-3B for the registered falsification).

\paragraph{The signed geometry.} At the peak, the language-family block
coheres (Qwen-3B: $+0.27$ to $+0.76$; Llama-3B: $+0.55$ to $+0.69$),
continent is anti-aligned with that block (Qwen $-0.07$ to $-0.20$; Llama
$-0.09$ to $-0.15$), sport-position is near-orthogonal to everything, and
country-of-origin sides with the block (strongly on Llama, $+0.51$ to
$+0.75$). The anti-alignment is confirmed by an independent statistic:
transfer from the continent axis into the language block falls below chance
on both families (Llama: AUC $0.20$--$0.33$); on Qwen the below-chance
transfer is bidirectional, on Llama one-directional, a partial replication
reported as such. Within-category transfer exceeds cross-category transfer
on both models, which also bears directly on the topic-generalization
question raised for the companion paper's protocol.

\paragraph{The reorganization with depth.} The early-block matrices are not
flat: they carry shared corpus statistics (the two families' early matrices
correlate at $r = 0.93$). Depth does not create arrangement from nothing; it
\emph{re-files} it. Citizenship sides with geography at the embedding level
and with the language family at the peak, on both families. The extreme
case: the axes of \emph{languages spoken} and \emph{official language} start
near anti-parallel ($\cos = -0.90$ Qwen, $-0.86$ Llama, presumably a lexical
opposition in how the two templates use language tokens) and merge at the
peak ($+0.76$, $+0.62$). Within each model, the early and peak off-diagonal
arrangements are uncorrelated or anti-correlated ($r = -0.55$ Qwen, $-0.18$
Llama): the peak structure contradicts, rather than inherits, the lexical
surface.

\paragraph{The FFN writes truth along category-dependent directions.}
Decoding the relation from the unit write direction $\Delta f$ at peak$+1$
($K{=}8$): $74.2\%$ (Qwen-3B) and $81.7\%$ (Llama-3B) against chance
$12.5\%$ and permutation null $95$th percentile $\approx 15.5\%$
($\hat{p} = 0.0099$ both); lexical baseline $39.2\%$/$42.1\%$. Thirty-five
to forty points of category structure in the write directions beyond token
identity. Read together with the relational law: the component that
overwrites truth frames does so along category-dependent directions. The
mixture is not only in the representation but in the writing.

\paragraph{The registered falsification and its verdict.} Five predictions
were written before any Llama measurement of this geometry; outcomes,
including the partials, in full. \textbf{R1} (language block on Llama,
signed cosines in $[+0.15, +0.45]$): structure confirmed, magnitude
\emph{outside} the registered window on the high side ($+0.55$ to $+0.69$).
\textbf{R2} (continent anti-aligned; below-chance transfer both
directions): signs confirmed; the bidirectional below-chance transfer held
only partially (one direction on Llama). \textbf{R3} ($\Delta f$ decoding
$\geq 10$ points over lexical, $p < 0.05$): confirmed, margin about $40$
points. \textbf{R4} (the citizenship early$\to$peak switch replicates):
confirmed, plus the stronger languages/official-language merge.
\textbf{R5} (the arrangement law): confirmed. Off-diagonal peak matrices,
Qwen-3B vs.\ Llama-3B: Pearson $r = +0.770$ (Spearman $0.893$), Mantel
category-label permutation null $95$th percentile $+0.498$, $p = 0.0009$;
robust to excluding the anomalous relation P176 ($r = +0.808$,
$p = 0.0037$).

\paragraph{The arrangement law.} Combined with the within-model
reorganization, the reading is: the two families share a lexical starting
arrangement, abandon it, and converge on the same functional arrangement.
Same start, same move, same destination, different machines. The
arrangement of the truth mixture is a property of the knowledge domain, not
of the training recipe: a signed, truth-specific instance of cross-model
representation convergence~\cite{platonic2024}. In hindsight this section
also explains a companion-paper measurement from the other side: forcing
one axis onto heterogeneous material costs $0.203$ of held-out AUC against
a full probe, versus $0.062$ on homogeneous material~\cite{companion},
because the single axis is being asked to summarize a signed mixture with
one number.

\paragraph{Anomaly, declared.} On Llama-3B, relation P176 (manufacturer)
shows within-CV and incoming transfer of $1.000$ from every axis: universal
perfect separability, which suggests surface structure in that relation's
pairs rather than truth geometry. It is excluded in the R5 robustness check
(which holds) and flagged for inspection before any claim rests on it.

\section{The measured dictionary and the sparse contract}
\label{sec:contract}

\texttt{dictionary\_export.py} saves, per model, the \emph{measured
dictionary}: the per-category oriented axes, the global axis, and the FFN
write centroids (a $[K \times d]$ bundle of tens of kilobytes), with signed
matrices and full metadata in one loadable file: a $K$-direction
truth/category monitor whose baseline performance is quantified above. The
sparse autoencoder that this series hands to engineering is justified only
if, against matched nulls, it (1) aligns with the per-category axes, (2)
respects the semantic geography, (3) reproduces the signs, including the
inverted loading on continent and the depth reorganization, and (4) beats
the measured dictionary on class-signal reconstruction and category
decoding. Either outcome is content.

\section{Leads, declared as leads}
\label{sec:leads}

\paragraph{A second readable dimension in the mid-knowledge regime.} On
Llama-3.2-1B at the peak, a held-out logistic on $(\mathrm{Re},
\mathrm{Im})$ reaches $0.783$ vs.\ $0.690$ for the single axis ($+0.092$);
the phase score alone reaches $0.755$; on Llama-3.2-3B phase scores match or
beat the axis by $+0.08$--$0.10$ in the decay zone. In the Qwen models the
second dimension never added more than $+0.02$. Read through the
dimensionality relation, this is the expected geometric signature of the
mid-knowledge regime: as knowledge decreases, truth de-concentrates and
off-axis components become readable. It refines, and does not contradict,
the one-dimensionality claim of~\cite{companion}, which was always scoped to
known facts. Status: no dedicated null on the 2D$-$1D margin yet.

\paragraph{Per-head anatomy.} The attention contribution is a sum of
$12$--$32$ heads through the output projection, and the fixed-axis
instrument applies unchanged to each head's slice. The open question is
whether the pro-truth propagation at the peak (and the 3B post-peak
tug-of-war) is carried by a few identifiable heads, connecting this anatomy
to the head-specialization taxonomy of~\cite{headgenome}, or distributed
across the pack.

\paragraph{Framing bias with an open lexical confound.} A $2{\times}2$
moral/prudential design ($24$ quads) on Qwen2.5-1.5B: unlike polarity,
framing does not rotate the axis (moral-to-prudential transfer
$0.979$--$0.99$; no flip), but there is a large level bias among
equally-true statements ($-2.75\sigma$ at layer $11$, stable through the
middle band), and the SVD of mixed-framing pairs recovers a framing
direction at $|\cos| = 0.912$ in PC2 (with $t_G$ in PC1 at $0.993$), the
same recovery structure as the companion paper's polarity result, on a new
factor. The confound: the bias is already $-2.86\sigma$ at layer $1$, which
shouts lexical surface (``wrong'' vs.\ ``risky''), not deep context.
Crossed-template controls are required before this is more than a lead.

\paragraph{Late-layer decoupling.} In all inspected models the total volume
written by the FFN grows toward the output (contribution norms $16 \to 38$
on the 1.5B; OV circuit norms exploding in the last blocks) while its
projection on the truth axis goes to zero and the intact truth AUC decays to
near chance. The terminal regime is not an oscillation of the axis but a
decoupling: the model writes enormously, and none of it speaks of truth.
Small terminal sign changes in the gap are zero-crossings of a dying signal
(below both the stability criterion and the permutation null) and must not
be read as structure.

\paragraph{Frame-aware re-reading of the split table.} The observational
gate/value split of Table~\ref{tab:split} was measured under a peak-fit
ruler and should be re-read against pre-FFN rulers; the causal freezes are
frame-free and the attribution of Sec.~\ref{sec:erosion} stands regardless.

\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limits}

Four models, still two families; a third family would promote the
arrangement law from law-candidate to law and widen the base of the
relational law. The pre ruler removes the direct containment circularity
but not indirect paths (accumulated earlier writes shape the pre-frame). The
relational law is measured on last-token states over one dataset
(CounterFact, pinned revision) with one statement form per relation
(template syntax may contribute to the continent anti-alignment; crossed
templates are the known cure). No permutation nulls were run on the
provenance matrices themselves; the diagonal magnitudes ($|d'|$ up to
$1.6$) sit far above the null scales measured on identical statistics, but
the formal check is future work. The value-stream attribution is causal on
one model; the corresponding runs on the 3B and the Llamas are mechanical to
add. Ablation measures necessity for linear readability of truth, not for
behavior; the gate/value freezes act at the reading position only. The
category section uses $60$ pairs per relation, no bootstrap error bars, two
weak within-axes on Qwen, and one degenerate relation on Llama (declared).
The know-rate is a string-match lower bound. The propagate/overwrite reading
is a description of measured alignments, not yet a circuit-level mechanism;
\emph{why} attention inherits frames and the FFN opposes them is the open
question this paper sharpens rather than answers.

\section{Conclusion}

Two movements, one instrument, one discipline. The mechanics: attention
propagates truth frames it did not write; the FFN opposes the frame of its
moment and deposits the material of the next; the genuine post-peak erosion
survives every control and is carried by the SwiGLU value stream. The
geometry: the single axis is a semantically signed mixture whose
arrangement the model does not inherit from the lexical surface but
constructs, and constructs the same way across two families, because the
arrangement belongs to the knowledge, not to the machine. Between the two
movements sits the correction that makes them trustworthy: a pre-registered
claim of ours, confirmed on three models, was exposed as the special case
of a better law by a control we built against our own instrument, and the
paper reports the failure with the same care as the findings, because a
result that has survived its owner's best attack is worth more than one
that has never been attacked. The handoff to the next stage is concrete: a
measured dictionary, a four-criterion contract for any sparse autoencoder
that claims to do better, and a per-head question with its instrument
already built.
