Knowledge-Dependent Dimensionality, a Relational Law, and a Convergent Category Geometry in Small Language Models

Small language models don’t store “true vs. false” as one global switch. They store it on a single measurable axis: which turns out to be a signed mixture of per-category directions, and the geometry of those axes is measurable, lawful, and shared across model families.

01 · ABSTRACT
The sixty-second version

The problem. A growing literature reports a single “truth direction” in the activations of language models, yet probes built on it often fail to transfer across content domains, and what that direction is made of has remained unclear.

The method. For eight Wikidata relations (mother tongue, official language, citizenship, continent…), matched true/false statements are run through Qwen2.5-3B and Llama-3.2-3B. At each depth, the difference of hidden states over each relation yields a per-category truth axis, recoverable label-free up to sign (Lemma 1). The signed cosines between the eight axes define the model’s truth geometry.

The findings. The geometry is knowledge-dependent, not lexical: axes that are near anti-parallel at the lexical block (cos −0.90) merge into one functional component at the readability peak (cos +0.76). A arrangement law holds across architectures: the two families arrange the same eight relations the same way at the peak (r = +0.770, Mantel p = 0.0009). And the geometry is written, not just readable: the FFN write direction after the peak decodes the category at 74–82% against a 12.5% chance floor.

02 · WHY IT WORKS
Four moves, label-free up to sign
I

PAIR

Ask the same fact twice , once true, once false. Everything else about the sentence stays identical, so the difference isolates truth, "the pairs are built knowing which is true; the estimator never sees which.

rows = H[true] − H[false]
II

DISTILL

One relation at a time, SVD finds the direction that difference lives on: a per-category truth axis, label-free up to sign.

axis = svd(rows).Vh[0]
III

COMPARE

The signed cosines between the eight axes give a geometry , who allies with whom, who opposes whom , at every depth of the network.

cos = axes @ axes.T
IV

CONVERGE

Do it in two unrelated model families. At the readability peak, their arrangements agree , same knowledge, same shape.

mantel(cos_qwen, cos_llama)
03 · KEY RESULTS
Hard numbers, not vibes
r = +0.770

THE ARRANGEMENT LAW

Two unrelated families , Qwen2.5-3B and Llama-3.2-3B , arrange the same eight relations the same way at the readability peak (Mantel p = 0.0009). Same knowledge, same geometry.

74% · 82%

THE FFN WRITES THE CATEGORY

The FFN write direction right after the peak decodes which relation is being processed: 74.17% (Qwen) and 81.67% (Llama) vs. a 39–42% lexical baseline and 12.5% chance (permutation p = 0.0099).

−0.90 → +0.76

THE MERGE

“Languages spoken” vs. “official language”: near anti-parallel at the lexical block, one component at the peak. The model re-files the concept , and both families make the same move.

8 × 2

LABEL-FREE AXES

Eight per-relation axes, two families, each recovered without labels up to sign (Lemma 1). Outliers are declared, not hidden: continent reads the others’ truth backwards; one Llama anomaly (P176) is excluded from robustness.
